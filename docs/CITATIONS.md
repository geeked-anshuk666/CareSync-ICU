# CareSync ICU — Medical Citations

All clinical claims in CareSync ICU are supported by peer-reviewed literature and established clinical guidelines.

---

## 1. ICU Handoff Communication Errors

**Claim:** Up to two-thirds of sentinel events reported to The Joint Commission are attributed to communication failures during patient handoffs, the majority occurring during ICU shift changes.

**Source:** Boat AC, Spaeth JP. "Handoff Communication." In: StatPearls. Treasure Island (FL): StatPearls Publishing; 2023.

**PubMed Central:** [PMC5898635](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5898635/)

**Relevance to CareSync:** Directly supports ShiftBrief — the agent designed to synthesize ICU shift handoffs and flag discrepancies.

---

## 2. Health Literacy and Informed Consent

**Claim:** Only 12% of US adults have health literacy sufficient to meaningfully understand informed consent documents and engage in complex medical decision-making.

**Source:** Simplifying Informed Consent as a Universal Precaution. Scientific Reports, 2024.

**DOI:** [10.1038/s41598-024-64139-9](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-64139-9)

**Relevance to CareSync:** Directly supports ConsentIQ — the agent that generates 6th grade reading level consent explanations with multilingual translation.

---

## 3. Discharge Medication Errors

**Claim:** Pharmacist-led medication reconciliation at discharge identified errors in 68% of patients, 75% classified as serious, and 35% capable of causing emergency visits or hospital readmissions.

**Source:** Impact of pharmacist-led discharge medication reconciliation on error and patient harm prevention. PubMed, 2024.

**PubMed ID:** [39697669](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39697669/)

**Relevance to CareSync:** Directly supports MedReconcile — the agent that compares pre-admission and discharge medication lists and flags dangerous interactions.

---

## 4. 30-Day Hospital Readmission

**Claim:** Suboptimal medication reconciliation at discharge is associated with 28% of potentially avoidable readmissions within 30 days.

**Source:** Decreasing Medication Errors at Discharge. PMC, 2022.

**PubMed Central:** [PMC9554890](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9554890/)

**Relevance to CareSync:** Directly supports ReadmitGuard — the agent that calculates 30-day readmission risk and generates personalized post-discharge care plans.

---

## 5. Clinical Alarm Fatigue

**Claim:** Alarm fatigue — when clinicians become desensitized to the large volume of monitor alarms — is a persistent and deadly problem in ICU settings, with false alarm rates estimated between 85 and 99%.

**Source:** The Joint Commission Sentinel Event Alert, Issue 50: Medical Device Alarm Safety in Hospitals. 2013.

**URL:** [Joint Commission Sentinel Event Alert Issue 50](https://www.jointcommission.org/resources/sentinel-event/sentinel-event-alert-newsletters/sentinel-event-alert-issue-50-medical-device-alarm-safety-in-hospitals/)

**Relevance to CareSync:** Directly supports AlertFatigue — the agent that contextualizes clinical alarms against patient-specific baselines to reduce false positive burden.

---

## 6. OR-to-ICU Handoff Risk

**Claim:** In analysis of OR-to-ICU handoffs, 81 process failures were identified, 22 of which were determined to be critical, with the highest-risk failures including lack of preliminary communication and team member absence.

**Source:** Operating Room to Intensive Care Unit Handoffs and the Risks of Patient Harm. PMC, 2015.

**PubMed Central:** [PMC4536086](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4536086/)

**Relevance to CareSync:** Supports the broader ShiftBrief and TriageIQ mission — handoff failures at the point of ICU admission are among the most dangerous in hospital medicine.

## Additional Supporting Evidence

**National CLAS Standards (Health Equity):**
The National Standards for Culturally and Linguistically Appropriate Services in Health and Health Care (National CLAS Standards) require healthcare organizations to provide language access services to individuals with limited English proficiency. ConsentIQ and FamilyBridge directly address this requirement.

Source: [U.S. Department of Health and Human Services — National CLAS Standards](https://www.thinkculturalhealth.hhs.gov/clas/standards)

**FDA Clinical Decision Support Guidance:**
CareSync ICU provides information synthesis and flagging — not diagnosis or treatment decisions. This positions it within FDA's 2022 Clinical Decision Support guidance as non-device software, requiring no premarket review.

Source: [FDA — Clinical Decision Support Software Guidance, 2022](https://www.fda.gov/media/153807/download)
